Features

Unlike the Decennial Census, the American Community Survey is an ongoing statistical survey administered by the U.S. Census Bureau. The survey results are used by government agencies to allocate over $400 billion of budget items. Results are aggregated in varying time frames, with aggregations ranging from one to five years.

  • Limitations
    • Results are aggregated
    • Limited sample size (relative to Decennial)
    • Margins of Error
  • Advantages
    • Small geographies available
    • Socioeconomic variables
    • Updated annually
    • Robust aging dataset
